# SliceMatic 2-Minute Pitch Script

Speaker notes for [`pitch/index.html`](index.html). **Target: 2:00 · ~280 words.**
Pace it calm and deliberate (~140 wpm) — let the visuals carry the weight, don't race.

**How to read this:** the blockquote is what you *say*, word for word. **Bold** = lean on it.
`(pause)` = one beat of silence. Advance the slide with `→` or space as you start each section.

---

### Slide 1 · Hook — `0:00–0:14`
*On screen: the phone mockup.*

> This is **SliceMatic** — a Delhi pizza outlet where every order still comes in over the phone.
> We turn that phone call into a **system**: it takes the order, gets the bill exactly right, and
> learns from every one. `(pause)` Same kitchen, same riders — a smarter business.

*Open confident and unhurried. This line sets your whole frame.*

---

### Slide 2 · The Problem — `0:14–0:32`
*On screen: the messy paper order ticket.*

> Today, that phone call **is** the problem. Orders get scribbled by hand and garbled. Discounts
> and GST are worked out in someone's head, so bills come out **wrong**. And nothing is recorded —
> the owner plans on memory. `(pause)` That's up to **ninety minutes a day**, lost to a notepad.

*Gesture at the crossed-out total. Let "wrong" land.*

---

### Slide 3 · The Solution — `0:32–0:51`
*On screen: the four-step pipeline.*

> Here's the fix: **one guided order, correct every time.** The customer orders in plain language —
> and soon, by **voice**. The AI only *understands* what they said. Our **code** does every rupee —
> the bulk discount, the eighteen-percent GST — so the bill is always exact, and every order is saved.

*This is the core idea. Hit the split: "AI understands, code computes."*

---

### Slide 4 · The Economics — `0:51–1:13`
*On screen: a slow ~6s reveal — the meter auto-flips **Before → Now** (Rs.644 kept), then the breakdown builds line-by-line below. Pace your words to it; tap the toggle to replay the contrast.*

> Look at a single order. **Eight-forty-seven** in revenue. By phone, that margin leaks at every
> step — wrong bills, missed orders. With our system, after ingredients, packaging, delivery and
> payments, **six-forty-four** stays. **Seventy-six percent.** `(pause)` We break even at **eleven
> orders a day**; we plan for forty-seven — more than **four times** over.

*Slow down — your strongest slide. Let the bar fill on "stays." Optionally tap **Phone ordering** to show the leak, then **SliceMatic** to fill it back.*

---

### Slide 5 · The Dashboard — `1:13–1:29`
*On screen: the admin dashboard mock.*

> And because every order is captured, the owner opens a **live dashboard** — total revenue, the
> top-selling pizza, the busiest hour, one-click export. `(pause)` Every order becomes a **decision**.

*Sweep a hand across the dashboard. "Every order becomes a decision" is the takeaway.*

---

### Slide 6 · The Toolkit — `1:29–1:43`
*On screen: the Build / Data / AI stack.*

> We're building it for real: **Next.js on Vercel**, **Supabase** for the database and login, and
> **OpenRouter** for the AI. The language layer understands; the **code** always does the money.

*Keep this brisk — it's the proof you can ship, not a tech lecture.*

---

### Slide 7 · The Vision (close) — `1:43–2:00`
*On screen: the roadmap + closing line.*

> We've finished the plan and a working prototype. Next is the **live product** — a conversational
> agent, then voice. `(pause)` SliceMatic started on the phone. **We're giving it back its voice —
> automated, accurate, and always learning.** `(pause)` Thank you.

*Land the last line. Stop. Don't trail off into "...so, yeah." Silence is strong.*

## Delivery reminders

- **Press F11** for fullscreen before you start.
- Numbers are your credibility — say them slowly and clearly (Slide 4 especially).
- Look at the judges, not the screen. The deck is the backup, you are the pitch.
- Practice once with a timer. If you're at 2:10, you're talking too fast, not over-scripted — breathe.
